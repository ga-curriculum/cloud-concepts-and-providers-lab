<h1>
  <span class="headline">Cloud Service Providers and Business Needs</span>
  <span class="subhead">How to Choose the Right Provider</span>
</h1>

**Learning objective:** By the end of this lesson, students will be able to think critically about what businesses might need from a cloud provider.

Choosing a cloud provider is highly dependent upon what's important to you or your business. Is it the price? The location? Certain capabilities?
Here are some things you might think about:

- **Cost:** How much can you afford to spend? Which provider offers the best value for your money?

- **Ease of Use:** How easy is it to start using the provider's services? Do they have good support and documentation?

- **Reliability:** How often do the provider's services go down? What happens if there's an outage?

- **Security:** How does the provider keep your data safe? Do they comply with any security standards?

- **Compatibility:** Does the provider work well with the technology you already use?

The answers to these questions will be different for every business or even for every situation. It's important to think about what matters most for your particular situation.

# Exercise

- Make a list of 3-5 things that would be most important for you or your business when choosing a cloud provider.

- For each item on your list, write a sentence or two explaining why it's important to you or your business.

- Based on what is important, research which provider would likely be the right choice and note this down.
